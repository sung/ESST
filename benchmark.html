Benchmarking

The performance of new ESSTs was benchmarked by using CRESCENDO [5], which is a program for predicting functional residues from a three dimensional structure. We benchmakrked new ESSTs based on the predictions of four categories of functional residues:
1) catalytic residues of enzyme active sites,
2) residues involved in protein-protein interactions,
3) protein-nucleic acid interactions and
4) protein-ligand interactions.

The benchmarking was designed to investigate following two questions.
(1) How well a new ESST can identify functional residues compared with the ESST of Shi et al. which is used as a default by CRESCENDO at the moment.
(2) If there is any improvement, what makes the improvement?

The divergent score was used as it is more sensitive to the environments and it better discriminates functionally conserved residues from structurally conserved residues. The CRESCENDO scores (Z-score) were smoothed and contoured using Kin3Dcont. CRESCENDO returns several clusters of predicted residues based on the size of grid points contoured using the Z-score. Residues only in the first cluster were used as the predicted residues of functional residues in the analysis. The details of the equation can be found in the original paper [5].
